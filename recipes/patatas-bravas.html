Spanish Patatas Bravas

I used to live in Spain and spent many of my teenage years in Spain eating Patatas Bravas.

For those of you that don't speak Spanish let me translate Patatas Bravas, it simply means brave potatoes.

Let's dive in and learn how to make Brave potatoes

Ingredients

Dipping Sauce

  • Garlic - 1 Clove
  • Paprika - 1/2 teaspoon
  • Salt - 1 Pinch
  • Mayonnaise - 1 Cup
  • Sherry Vinegar - 1 Tablespoon
  • Tomato Paste - 1 Teaspoon
  • Ground Chipotle Peppers - 1/4 Teaspoon
  • Cayenne Pepper - 1 Pinch

Spice Blend

  • Salt - 1 Teaspoon
  • Ground Black Pepper - 1/2 Teaspoon
  • Paprika - 1/2 Teaspoon
  • Ground Chipotle Peppers - 1/2 Teaspoon

Potatoes

  • Cold Water - 2 Quarts
  • Salt - 1 Teaspoon
  • Smoked Paprika - 1 Teaspoon
  • Bay Leaves - 2
  • Russet Potatoes - 2 Pounds peeled and cut into squares
  • Vegetable oil - 2 Cups

Directions

  1. Making the dipping sauce: Combine garlic, smoked paprika and salt in a mortar process until smooth.

    Mix in mayonnaise, sherry vinegar, tomato paste, chipotle powder, and cayenne pepper until combined.

  2. Make spice blend: Mix all seasoning and set aside
  3. Make potatoes: Pour water into a large saucepan. Stir salt, smoked paprika

    cumin, and bay leaves into water. Bring to boil reduce heat and cover.

  4. Stir potatoes into seasoned water, bring to boil, and simmer until potatoes are tender.

    Drain potatoes and leave to cool on wire rack for 45 minutes

  5. Heat oil, add potatoes stirring frequently, until golden brown. Around 5 to 6

    minutes. Transfer potatoes to a baking sheet lined with paper towels to cool

  6. Toss in a bowl with seasoning and serve with dip.
